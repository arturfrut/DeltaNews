Finding the "best" credit card depends on your spending habits and financial goals. Here are some top contenders in different categories to get you started:

* Best for travel rewards:
  * Chase Sapphire Preferred® Card: This card offers 2x points on travel and dining and has a $95 annual fee.[creditcards.chase.com](https://creditcards.chase.com/rewards-credit-cards/sapphire/preferred) Chase Sapphire Preferred® Card 
  * Capital One Venture X Rewards Credit Card: This card offers a variety of travel perks, including airport lounge access and travel credit, and has a $395 annual fee.[www.capitalone.com](https://www.capitalone.com/credit-cards/venture-x/) Capital One Venture X Rewards Credit Card 
* Best for cash back:
  * Wells Fargo Active Cash® Card: This card offers a flat 2% cash back on all purchases and has no annual fee. [www.creditkarma.com](https://www.creditkarma.com/credit-cards/insights/wells-fargo-active-cash-card) Wells Fargo Active Cash® Card 
  * Blue Cash Everyday® Card from American Express: This card offers up to 3% cash back on groceries and gas, and has a $0 or $95 annual fee depending on the card you choose.[www.americanexpress.com](https://www.americanexpress.com/us/credit-cards/card/blue-cash-everyday/) Blue Cash Everyday® Card from American Express 
* Best for balance transfers:
  * Citi® Diamond Preferred® Card: This card offers a 0% intro APR on balance transfers for 21 months (then 18.25% - 28.25% APR), and has a $95 annual fee.[www.citi.com](https://www.citi.com/credit-cards/citi-diamond-preferred-credit-card) Citi® Diamond Preferred® Card 
* Best for students:
  * Discover it® Student chrome℠ Card: This card offers cash back match for the first year, has a $0 annual fee, and is designed for students with limited credit history.[www.discover.com](https://www.discover.com/credit-cards/student-credit-card/chrome-card.html) Discover it® Student chrome℠ Card 

Here are some additional tips for choosing the best credit card for you:

* Consider your credit score. Some cards have higher credit score requirements than others.
* Think about your spending habits. Choose a card that rewards the types of purchases you make most often.
* Pay attention to the annual fee. Some cards have annual fees, while others do not. Decide if the rewards are worth the fee.
* Read the fine print. Make sure you understand all of the terms and conditions of the card before you apply.
